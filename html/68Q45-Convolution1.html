convolution


Let Σ be an alphabet, # a symbol not in Σ.

Let x1⁢x2⁢…⁢x|x|,y1⁢y2⁢…⁢y|y|,z1⁢z2⁢…⁢z|z|,… be n words over Σ*. Let l denote the maximum length.

The convolution of these words is

(x1,y1,…)⁢(x2,y2,…)⁢…⁢(xl,yl,…)

where for any i>|w|, the wi is #. This is a new word in ((Σ∪{#})n)*.

The convolution of x,y,z,… is sometimes denoted conv(x,y,z,…), or x⋆y⋆z⋆…

Example

The convolution of a⁢n⁢d,f⁢i⁢s⁢h,b⁢e is

(a,f,b)⁢(n,i,e)⁢(d,s,#)⁢(#,h,#)

Notes

This definition bears no to the notion of convolution (http://planetmath.org/Convolution) of functions.
