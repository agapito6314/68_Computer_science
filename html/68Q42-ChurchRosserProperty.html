Church-Rosser property


Let → be a reduction (a binary relation) on a set S, and let ↔* be the reflexive transitive symmetric closure of →. The reduction → is said to have the Church-Rosser property provided that a↔*b implies that a and b are joinable, for any a,b∈S.

In terms of diagrams, the Church-Rosser property means the following, for any a,b∈S, if

a↔x1↔x2↔⋯↔xn↔b

where u↔v means u→v or u←v (:=v→u), then there is some x∈S such that

a→a1⁢⋯→ap→x←bq←⋯←b1←b.

Remark. It can be shown that → has the Church-Rosser property iff it is confluent.
